Canine Kerfuffle

  • High Concept: Doggy momentum You control a dog with slippery momentum.
  • Player Experience Goals: Casual Little experience required.
  • Genre: Platformer The mechanics are intended for the platforming genre.
  • Game play: The player controls a dog who, once moved, continues to slide. Compounding left or right input (by holding left or right down) builds momentum. The player can jump which starts at a high velocity, slows down as they approach maximum jump height, falls at a low yet increasing velocity, and ends when the player has returned to the surface.
  • Prototype goal: Fine tuning momentum and jumping mechanics.
  • Features: The effort put into momentum makes player movement less rigid and predictable. By making the controls slightly unwieldy, the player has a skill to master.
  • Setting/Premise: The player controls a slippery pup attempting to find his owners. He is lost in a field and his panic has hastened his pace. He wants to find his owners as quickly as possible.
  • Story: The dog is lost and scared, reaching for the strand of hope that he can be reunited with his loved ones.
  • Target Audience: Everyone who likes dogs.
  • Play time: Only a few minutes, just enough time for a quick challenge.
  • Strategies: Learning to control the dog's momentum through practice.
  • Prototype Assets: I studied the phaser game Starstruck to understand movement.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Super Mario Bros. due to it's momentum, but Starstruck from phaser is a clear inspiration.
